PlaceIQ Privacy Statement

  1. General
PlaceIQ, Inc. and its affiliates (collectively, “we,” “our,” “us,” or “PlaceIQ”) own and operate www.PlaceIQ.com (the “Site”) and we are committed to protecting your privacy and Personal Data (as defined below) about you. This Privacy Statement is intended to describe our practices regarding information that identifies, relates to, describes, is reasonably capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, with a particular individual or a household (collectively “Personal Data.”). Personal Data may relate to visitors to the Site, our customers and potential customers, and individuals whose Device Data (as defined below) we may process (“you” or “your”) in connection with our advertising, marketing, and geolocation data products and services (collectively the “Services”), for what purposes Personal Data may be used, and with whom we may share Personal Data.

2. What Personal Data Do We Collect? When You Communicate with Us When you ask to be contacted or have questions about our products and services, we collect the following identifiers that may, alone or in combination with other information, constitute Personal Data:
  • Name;
  • Phone number;
  • Content of communications sent to us;
  • E-mail address; and
  • Email preferences.
You have no obligation to provide the Personal Data described above, but if you don’t provide such Personal Data, we may not be able to communicate with you.

(ii) Internet Activity Information

Software used on our website (the “Site”) collects Internet or other electronic network activity information that may, alone or in combination with other information, constitute Personal Data:
  1. The domain from which you access the Site;
  2. The type of browser and operating system used in the device you use to access the Site;
  3. Internet Protocol (IP) address;
  4. The date and time you visited the Site (time stamp);
  5. The pages browsed on the Site;
  6. The website used to link to the Site from;
  7. Any search terms used to navigate the Site;
  8. A browsing session ID; and
  9. Whether you are a first-time visitor to the Site.
For additional information about cookies used on the Site, please review Section 9. below.

Google Analytics is an element of the Site. By using cookies, Google Analytics collects and stores data such as time of visit, pages visited, time spent on each page of the Site, the Internet Protocol address, and the type of operating system used in the devices used to access the Site. Google Analytics does not share Internet Protocol addresses with us. GA4, which will fully replace Universal Analytics in July 2023, will not collect Internet Protocol addresses. By using a browser plugin available at http://www.google.com/ads/preferences/plugin/ provided by Google, you can opt out of Google Analytics.

(iii) Our Data Products and Services

In connection with our Services, we receive data that was initially collected (over time and across websites, mobile apps, automotive data systems, and other devices) by our data partners (such as mobile app publishers and developers, advertising networks, automotive data aggregators, and ad exchanges and other websites and services). This data may, alone or in combination with other information, constitute Personal Data and includes:

  • “Device Data” including:
    • Geolocation based on latitude and longitude of a mobile device or vehicle;
    • A unique device identifier such as an advertising identifier;
    • Other information about a device (such as the browser or device type, IP address or app publisher, operating system, carrier provider, and other similar device-specific information);
  • Inferences drawn from Device Data;
  • Demographic data;
  • Transactional purchase data; and
  • So-called ID graphs that are generated based on interactions with products, mobile apps, and websites and which connect user behaviors to various identifiers including, not by way of limitation, advertising identifiers, cookie data, households, hashed emails and IP addresses (collectively “ID Graphs”).
If you want to limit or prevent Device Data from your mobile device from being collected, you can adjust this capability through your mobile device settings. Instructions on how to manage your location settings are available for current Android devices  https://support.google.com/nexus/answer/6179507?hl=en&ref_topic=6179522, and for iOS devices https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT207092.

We do not associate the geolocation of a device or other Personal Data processed by us in connection with our Services with the following types of sensitive locations:

  • Places of worship such as locations in use as gatherings for purposes of religious worship;
  • Correctional facilities;
  • Places that identify or suggest a LGBTQ+ sexual preference;
  • Places that identify or suggest a sexual interest;
  • Places which are predominantly occupied by children under 16 (e.g., nursery school, elementary and middle school, day care center);
  • Domestic abuse shelters, such as rape crisis centers and shelters for abused or battered men, women or children;
  • Social service centers such as homeless shelters and halfway houses;
  • Dependency treatment such as alcohol, gambling or drug treatment centers;
  • Locations where firearms are sold or used (e.g., gun dealer, shooting range) excluding general sporting and retail locations;
  • Medical facilities that cater predominantly to sensitive conditions, such as cancer centers, abortion and/or family planning clinics, HIV/Aids, planned parenthood, mental health treatment facilities or emergency room trauma centers;
  • Military bases (including recruitment centers located on military bases), first responder locations such as fire department or police department; and
  • Places associated with services for marginalized groups including refugee centers and immigrant services.
Please visit the Network Advertising Initiative for additional information about voluntary Enhanced Standards for Sensitive Points of Interest requirements.

We use Personal Data about you for the following purposes:

    6. To conduct cross-device identity matching;
    7. To provide insights and data to our third-party partners for various commercial purposes;
    8. To send you direct marketing messages;
    9. To develop sales leads;
    10. To protect against, identify and prevent fraud and other unlawful activity, claims and other liabilities;
    11. To comply with applicable legal requirements, industry standards, and our own policies; and
    12. Where permitted by law, to provide you with information about other products and services we offer that are similar to those that you have already subscribed to or enquired about and we feel may interest you.

6. California Privacy Rights

(i) Introduction

California Civil Code Section 1798.83 permits California residents to request certain information regarding our disclosure of Personal Data to third parties for their direct marketing purposes. To make such a request, please contact us at privacy@PlaceIQ.com. Please note we extend California Privacy Rights to all U.S. residents. If you are a California resident, you may also have the right:
      • To request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of Personal Data collected about you;
      • To request the disclosure of the business purpose for collecting or selling Personal Data; the categories of third parties with whom it is shared, and the categories of sources from which Personal Data is collected;
      • To request the deletion of Personal Data, subject to the limitations set forth in California Civil Code Section 1798.105(d); and
      • Not to be discriminated against for exercising the rights guaranteed by California Civil Code Section 1798.100 et seq.
For a list of categories of Personal Data collected and the purposes for the processing of that Personal Data, please refer to Sections 2 and 4 above. 

(ii) Requests to Know

You have the right to request that we disclose:
  1. The categories of Personal Data we collect;
  2. The categories of Personal Data we have sold or disclosed for a business purpose;
  3. The categories of sources from which we collect Personal Data about you;
  4. Our business or commercial purpose for selling or collecting Personal Data;
  5. The categories of Personal Data sold or shared about you, as well as the categories of third parties to whom the Personal Data was sold, by category of Personal Data for each party to whom information was sold;
  6. The specific pieces of Personal Data collected about you.
Delivery may take place electronically or by mail. We are not required to respond to requests relating to Personal Data more than twice in a 12-month period.

(iii) Requests to Delete

With certain exceptions, you have the right to request that we delete any Personal Data we have collected about you. Upon receiving a verified request to delete Personal Data about you, we will do so unless otherwise authorized by law.

(iv) Verifiable Requests

We will acknowledge the receipt of requests to know or requests to delete Personal Data free of charge, within ten (10) business days. In order to protect your privacy and the security of Personal Data, we may verify your request by asking you to provide additional Personal Data for us to verify your identity. We will respond to your request within 45 calendar days of receipt, provided that we have been able to successfully verify your identity.

You may submit a request to know or a request to delete Personal Data via privacy@PlaceIQ.com.

(v) Sale and Sharing of Personal Data

In connection with the Services, we may sell or otherwise share or make available Device Data to our customers and partners. Our sharing of Personal Data in this way may constitute a “sale” or “sharing” of Personal Data under the California Consumer Privacy Act (“CCPA”), as amended from time to time.

Pursuant to the CCPA, in the preceding 12 months, we have sold and/or shared the following categories of Personal Data:
  • Device Data, including geolocation history;
  • Internet or other similar network activity; and
  • Demographic data.
