Your Privacy Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have certain rights in relation to the processing of your personal data or personal information. These rights may include:

  1. The right to access your personal information (including a data portability request);
  2. The right to correct or amend any personal information we have on file about you;
  3. The right to delete your personal information;
  4. The right to limit the use of your “sensitive” personal information;
  5. The right to opt-out of the sale or “sharing” of your personal information;
  6. The right to opt-out of the use of your personal information for targeted advertising purposes;
  7. The right to restrict or object to the processing of your personal information (such as for direct marketing purposes);
  8. The right to restrict the use of your personal information for certain automated decision-making (including profiling); and
  9. The right to revoke your consent (to the extent applicable).

All of these rights are subject to certain exceptions under applicable law.

To exercise any of these rights, please fill out the webform below or contact us by email at privacyofficer@iqvia.com with your name, physical address, email address, the email address associated with your interactions with IQVIA (if different), telephone number, and details about your interactions with IQVIA (including whether you are a patient or a health care professional). You may also designate an authorized agent to submit a request on your behalf. To designate an agent, please also send us a written and signed document by both you and the agent that authorizes the agent to act on your behalf. You may also use a power of attorney. Please note that we will still require you to provide sufficient information to allow us to reasonably verify that you are the person about whom we are receiving a request. We will respond to any request you submit in the time period required by applicable law.

To learn more about how we collect, use, and disclose your personal information, please review the IQVIA Privacy Policy and the IQVIA Online Privacy Policy. If you are a California resident, please visit our California Privacy Notice for additional information that may apply to you.

Your Rights:

I WISH TO EXERCISE THE FOLLOWING RIGHTS:

You may have the right to access the personal information that we collect about you. Depending on the jurisdiction, we may be required to provide this information to you (to the extent technically feasible) in an electronic format that is commonly used, is easily understandable to the average consumer, and can be transferred to another entity at your request without hindrance. You may also have the right to request details regarding the information that we collect about you, including the categories of personal information that we collect, the sources of this personal information, our purposes for collecting and using this personal information, and the entities to which we disclose this personal information.

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to request a copy of the data we have collected about you in a portable manner. The purpose of this request is to allow you to obtain and reuse your personal data for your own purposes across different services.

You may have the right to delete personal information that we have collected about you. Depending on the jurisdiction, upon receiving a request for deletion, we may be obligated to request that service providers, contractors, and other third parties delete your personal information, as well. However, our obligation to honor your request to delete may be limited where our continued processing of your personal information is necessary for such purposes as: complying with a legal obligation; establishing, exercising, or defending legal claims; engaging in public or peer-reviewed scientific, historical, or statistical research; exercising the rights of freedom of speech, freedom of expression and information, and other rights provided for by law; fulfilling a contract between you and our business; and carrying out tasks in the public interest.

You may have the right to correct or rectify inaccurate or incomplete personal information that we have on file about you. Such correction or rectification may take into account the nature of the personal information and our purposes for processing it.

You may have the right to limit our use of your “sensitive” personal information. “Sensitive” personal information may include information revealing your racial or ethnic origin, religious or philosophical beliefs, political opinions, or union membership; genetic data; biometric data for the purpose of uniquely identifying you; information concerning your health, sex life, or sexual orientation; information revealing your precise geolocation; and information revealing certain sensitive identification numbers (e.g., social security number, passport number) or financial information (e.g., bank account number in combination with a password). Depending on the jurisdiction, and subject to various exceptions, we may be prohibited from processing your sensitive personal information or limited to processing your sensitive personal information to the extent necessary to perform the services or provide the goods reasonably expected by an average consumer who requests those goods or services.

You may have the right to withdraw your consent for, or opt-out of, our sale or sharing of your personal information at any time. Depending on the jurisdiction, a “sale” might encompass our business selling, renting, releasing, disclosing, disseminating, making available, transferring, or otherwise communicating orally, in writing, or by electronic or other means, your personal information to a third party for monetary or other valuable consideration. Relatedly, “sharing” might entail our business sharing, renting, releasing, disclosing, disseminating, making available, transferring, or otherwise communicating orally, in writing, or by electronic or other means, your personal information to a third party for cross-context behavioral advertising, whether or not for monetary or other valuable consideration, including transactions between our business and a third party for cross-context behavioral advertising for the benefit of a business in which no money is exchanged.

You may have the right to withdraw your consent for, or opt-out of, our use of your personal information for purposes of targeted advertising. Depending on the jurisdiction, targeted advertising may entail any advertisement that is selected for you based on personal information obtained or inferred over time from your activities over nonaffiliated websites, applications, or services.

You may have the right to restrict or object to the processing of your personal information. Depending on the jurisdiction, these rights may apply in such situations as: when your personal information is used for direct marketing purposes; when you have contested the accuracy of the personal information; when the processing is unlawful; or when the information is being processed for the performance of a task in the public interest.

You may have the right to restrict the use of your personal information by an automated decision-making system or process that produces legal or similarly significant effects on you (including profiling). An automated decision-making system is a method, model, or process that relies on algorithms, machine learning, or other artificial intelligence techniques to make decisions or facilitate human decisions, often by generating scores, predictions, and classifications. “Profiling” refers to the automated processing of your personal information to evaluate, analyze, or predict aspects of your life, including your work performance, economic situation, health, personal preferences, interests, reliability, behavior, location, or movements.

You may have the right to revoke your consent to our processing of your personal information at any time. Depending on the jurisdiction and situation, we may rely on your consent to process your personal information generally, to process your personal information for the purposes of targeted advertising or sale, to process your sensitive personal information, or to process your personal information for purposes that are not necessary to or compatible with the stated purposes for which we collected your personal information.

Strictly Necessary Cookies

Always Active

These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you which amount to a request for services, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in or filling in forms.    You can set your browser to block or alert you about these cookies, but some parts of the site will not then work. These cookies do not store any personally identifiable information.

Performance Cookies

These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us to know which pages are the most and least popular and see how visitors move around the site.    All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore anonymous. If you do not allow these cookies we will not know when you have visited our site, and will not be able to monitor its performance.

Functional Cookies

These cookies enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalisation. They may be set by us or by third party providers whose services we have added to our pages.    If you do not allow these cookies then some or all of these services may not function properly.

Targeting Cookies

These cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners. They may be used by those companies to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant adverts on other sites.    They do not store directly personal information, but are based on uniquely identifying your browser and internet device. If you do not allow these cookies, you will experience less targeted advertising.
